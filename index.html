The space

Small enough to feel like yours by the second morning.

The apartment is on an upper floor of a well-kept brick building from the late 1960s, with a lift and big windows that pull in the long eastern light. Itäharju is an easygoing, green part of Turku — quiet to come home to, but a few minutes from everything around Kupittaa.

The living space opens onto a compact kitchen with everything you'd actually cook with — a real oven, good knives, a stovetop espresso pot. The bedroom is quiet and dark when you want it to be, with blackout curtains for the bright summer nights and extra blankets for winter.

Kupittaa Park, the city's biggest, is a short walk away, and the train station beside it puts Helsinki under two hours by rail. The riverside old town is about ten minutes by bike or bus when you want the restaurants and the water.

The neighbourhood

Next to Kupittaa, minutes from the old town.

Itäharju sits just east of Kupittaa, Turku's green and lively student quarter. Kupittaa Park and the university campuses are a short walk away, the centre and the Aura River are a quick bike ride or bus, and the motorway and station make the rest of Finland easy to reach.
